Hi, I'm Shannon

I am a user experience designer currently pursuing my Master’s in Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon University, expecting to graduate in August 2017.

My Background

Before pursuing my master’s degree at Carnegie Mellon, I studied at Lafayette College, graduating in 2013 with a Bachelor’s of Science in Computer Science and minor in Psychology. After that, I worked for 3 years at Lutron Electronics, starting as a mixed front-end/server-side API software developer and fully transitioning to a user experience role around a year in after uncovering my passion and talent for user experience design. I went back to school to build upon the skills I had acquired while on the job and from studying human-computer interaction theory on my own time.

My Interests

My interest in user experience design comes from my desire to both understand and create. Both are equally important to develop successfully impactful design solutions.

I enjoy exploring new domains through rigorous research: reading, interviewing, and observing. The most rewarding aspect of research is teasing out overarching themes and patterns between various information sources and gaining insights into how everything is connected.

Achieving that understanding by itself is not enough though. I take ownership of the whole product lifecycle by applying my understanding to identify opportunities for improvement and create solutions that elegantly build upon these opportunities. I enjoy generating a wide range of ideas and iteratively improving them to end with a highly polished, well thought out solution.

Beyond UX

When I’m not out in the field doing research or ideating and implementing new design ideas, you can find me obsessively posting pictures of my (family’s) dog to Instagram, discovering new music on Spotify, watching and analyzing movies, and critiquing typography on everyday media.

My funniest hobby is probably playing podcasts and YouTube videos at half speed, purely for laughs.
